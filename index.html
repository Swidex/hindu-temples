HINDUISM

ARCHITECTURE IN TEMPLES

MAP

MANDAPAS

मण्डप


Definition

Meaning "pavillion", the mandapa is public gathering place for temples.

Architecture

The Mandapa is a pillared outdoor hall typically used for public rituals. It is a porch-like structure that leads to the temple. A large temple may have many mandapas. If a temple has many mandapas, they will be allocated for a different purpose and given a unique name. Marriage, dancing, and music are a few of the purposes for a mandapa.

GARBHAGRIHA

गर्भगृह


Definition

Translated literally, Garbhagriha means "womb chamber" in sanskrit. "garbha" meaning womb and "griha" for hour. The Grabhagriha is found in the innermost chamber of the temple.

Architecture

The room is a window-less shrine where he main deity featured in the temple is contained in the Garbhagriha, typically as a murti (icon). It is seen as if the spiritual energy radiates from the center, the garbhagriha, out in all directions. The area is often restricted to the "pujari" or priests of the temple.

The garbhagriha is often square and is placed upon a plinth, support of statues. The center of the temple is calculated and the main deity statue is placed upon the center. The placement in the center represents total equilibrium and the Universe.

History

Early temples used to only contain a garbhagriha, with no other building with it. By the 10th century CE, a style of the center being the garbhagriha, surrounded by mandapas developed.

SHIKHARA

ಶಿಖರ


Definition

Meaning "mountain peak", the Shikhara is the tower or spire above the mandapas.

Architecture

The Shikhara is the most dominant and apparent feature of Hindu temples. There are two types of shikhara, the latina and phamsana.

The latina contains rows of slabs, eventually merging into the center at the top. The latina shikhara is topped with a large grooved disk, the amalasararka.

ADHISTHANA/JAGATI

अधिष्ठान


Definition

Meaning "mountain peak", the Shikhara is the tower or spire above the mandapas.

Architecture

The raised base on which a temple was built. These are built tall, to parallel the temple to a mountain. Mountains are often seen as the home/dwelling of God.

The base is often ornamented with statues, moulding, or other structures. For example, the Gajendra peeth adhisthana has elephants to represent stories and legends of elephants with nature, humands, and God. As well as to honor the gentle animals that elephants are.

AMALAKA

आमलक


Definition

Meaning "dome", the amalaka is the disk on top of the shikhara.

Architecture

The amalaka is a large, groved disk on top of the shikhara. The amalaka represents the seat of the deity of the temple. It can also be interpreted as the sun, which is the entry to the heavenly world.

The amalaka is crowned with a kalasha, a metal pot. The kalasha is often depicted in Hindu iconography, and filled with coins, grains, gold, or a combination of this.
In Hinduism, the kalasha is seen as the "source of life" and believed to contain amrita, the elixir of life.

ANTARALA

अन्तराल


Definition

Meaning "intermediate space", the antarla is the space between the mandapas and the garbhagriha.

Architecture

The shape of the garbhagriha is always rectangular, never a square court. Only included in the larger temples, often excluded from most temples entirely.

The transition between the main hall and garbhagriha represents the transition from the earthly world to the divine world.

GENERAL


Colors

Saffron - Most sacred color. Represents purity and religious abstinence.

Red - Indicated sensuality and purity. In religion, it is most frequently used for marriages, birth, festivals, etc...

Yellow - Represents knowledge and learning. Symbolizes happiness, peace, meditation, and development.

Green - Festive color. Represents life and happiness in Maharashtra.

Blue - Represents the deities. The blue sky represents the color as it is the closest to God. Gods are colored with blue skin.

Styles

There are two main styles of temple architecture: Dravida and Nagara

Dravida:
   -   Multistory shikhara
   -   Doesn't include mandapa spires
   -   Straight-edged, pyramidal Shikhara
   -   Generally found in southeast Asia.

Nagara:
   -   Shikhara above sanctum.
   -   Includes mandapa spires.
   -   Curvilinear centered curvature of Shikhara.
   -   Found in northern, western, and central India.
