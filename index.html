GARBHAGRIHA

गर्भगृह


Definition

Translated literally, Garbhagriha means "womb chamber" in sanskrit. "garbha" meaning womb and "griha" for hour. The Grabhagriha is found in the innermost chamber of the temple.

Architecture

The room is a window-less shrine where he main deity featured in the temple is contained in the Garbhagriha, typically as a murti (icon). It is seen as if the spiritual energy radiates from the center, the garbhagriha, out in all directions. The area is often restricted to the "pujari" or priests of the temple.

The garbhagriha is often square and is placed upon a plinth, support of statues. The center of the temple is calculated and the main deity statue is placed upon the center. The placement in the center represents total equilibrium and the Universe.

History

Early temples used to only contain a garbhagriha, with no other building with it. By the 10th century CE, a style of the center being the garbhagriha, surrounded by mandapas developed.

SHIKHARA

ಶಿಖರ


Definition

Meaning "mountain peak", the Shikhara is the tower or spire above the mandapas.

Architecture

The Shikhara is the most dominant and apparent feature of Hindu temples. There are two types of shikhara, the latina and phamsana.

The latina contains rows of slabs, eventually merging into the center at the top. The latina shikhara is topped with a large grooved disk, the amalasararka.
